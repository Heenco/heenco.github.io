> Platform: instagram-reel-9x16 | Duration: 30s | Audience: osm-power-user

[SECTION 1 — HOOK 0:00–0:05]
Overture Maps has 64 million places worldwide. More than OSM in many regions. And it's all free.

[SECTION 2 — THE PROBLEM 0:05–0:12]
But getting a subset of it — say, all cafes in a specific district — normally means DuckDB SQL or a Python CLI with your bbox coordinates figured out separately.

[SECTION 3 — THE FIX 0:12–0:25]
With the Overture Downloader you draw the area on the map, select Places, and download. You get all 64M categorised POIs filtered to your bounding box, as GeoJSON. Ready for JOSM, QGIS, or whatever you're running your conflation workflow in.
